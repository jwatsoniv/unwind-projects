WEEK 1 — Orientation, Systems Introduction & First Inspections April 14–18
DAY 1 — Tuesday, April 14  |  Orientation

Who We Are

  • 55 properties now, growing to 90+ by end of 2026. Every new property adds to her operation.
  • Properties are owned by individuals — investors, families, vacation home owners. We manage on their behalf. If a property is dirty, it directly affects public reviews.
  • Two booking platforms: Airbnb and VRBO. Both post public cleanliness scores. Target: 4.8+ stars on cleanliness. A 4.8 out of 5 means 96% of guests give 5 stars — it all starts with the condition of the property when the guest walks in.
  • The cleaning operation is completely decentralized: different locations, different layouts, different owner standards.

About the Cleaning Team

  • All cleaners are 1099 contractors — independent, not employees. Contractors choose their jobs. If they don't want to do a property, they can say no.
  • Most cleaners speak Spanish or Portuguese. Google Translate is standard — she should be comfortable using it immediately.
  • The cleaners have been under a manager who was burned out and negative. Build relationships first, set standards second.
  • Some cleaners may have complaints or loyalty to the previous manager. Listen, don't react. Don't draw conclusions about a cleaner's reliability for at least 30 days.
The message to communicate to every cleaner she meets: "I'm not here to change everything. I'm here to make this easier for all of us. I want clean communication, fair work, and for you to know that when you show up, you'll be treated well. Tell me what you need."

CLEANER COVERAGE ASSESSMENT — What to Deliver to John & Matt
By end of Week 2, she should be able to answer: How many cleaners do we have? How many do we need? Where are the gaps?

How to Assess Each Cleaner's Capacity

Work with each cleaner individually to understand how many properties they can realistically handle per day while keeping quality high. There is no universal number — it depends on property size, drive time, and the individual. Find the right load for each person.

Peak Season Reality: During June–August, every property may turn 2–3 times per week. She needs enough cleaners to handle that load — not just today's volume. Build the team now, not in June.

How to Find New Cleaners (When Needed)

  • Ask existing cleaners — word of mouth in the Spanish/Portuguese-speaking community is the most reliable channel
  • Facebook groups for local cleaning services
  • Ask John what channels have worked in the past

What to Report to John & Matt (End of Week 2)

  • Number of active contractors currently reliable and assigned
  • Number of properties without reliable coverage right now
  • Number of additional cleaners needed before peak season
  • Any contractor relationships that feel unstable or at risk
  • Her plan to recruit the new cleaners she's identified as needed
