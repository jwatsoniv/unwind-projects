✓ No application, no debt
✓ Earns 4.5% APY in HYSA

✗ Requires saving $47K in 5 months
✗ No safety net if summer underperforms

Option 2 — Business Line of Credit
~$2,200–2,800
Estimated interest cost (5 mo. avg draw of ~$45K at 9.5%)
✓ No cash tied up all summer
✓ Draw only what you need, when needed
✓ Revolving — resets each summer
✓ Safety net if summer underperforms
✗ Interest expense (~$2,500/yr)
✗ Qualification requires 2+ yrs, financials
✗ Lender may restrict or call line
Best sources: Local community bank or credit union (best rates). SBA CAPLine (gov-backed working capital). Avoid Bluevine/Fundbox — rates 12–18%+.

Option 3 — Hybrid Strategy
Best of both
Save aggressively + open LOC as backstop only
✓ Save 30% of net Apr–Sep into HYSA
✓ Target $75K+ in HYSA by Oct 1
✓ Open $50K–75K LOC now (before you need it)
✓ LOC sits unused = $0 cost most years
✓ Draw LOC only if HYSA falls short
Key insight: Apply for the LOC before you need it — banks lend when you don't need the money. With a profitable 2026 summer, you may never draw it. But it protects against a slow July or unexpected expense spike.
